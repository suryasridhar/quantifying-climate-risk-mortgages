# Quantifying Climate Risk in Residential Mortgages
With climate change and its risks increasingly affecting residential areas, it’s important for lenders and GSEs to anticipate how mortgages might behave in places like coastal zones, floodplains, and wildfire-prone regions. While future losses can sometimes be observed through insurance payouts after climate-related disasters, this project focuses on modeling and quantifying the lead-lag relationship between climate exposure and mortgage losses before disasters happen. We use publicly available climate data to generate new input features for models, back test them against how mortgages performed in past events and simulate potential scenarios to better understand market dynamics. This approach is especially relevant from a GSE standpoint, as research shows that more climate-prone mortgages are being securitized.
